Norsk Oljemuseum (Norwegian Petroleum Museum)

Website: https://www.norskolje.museum.no/en/
Google Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Norsk%20Oljemuseum%2C%20Kjeringholmen%201A%2C%204006%20Stavanger
Open: Daily 10:00-19:00 (Jun 1 - Aug 31)
Walk: 1.0 km
route in Google Maps
Walk steps: Down Lokkeveien to Arne Rettedals gt, through Haakon VIIs gate, across Kongsgata to Kirkegata, descend Breigata to Havneringen and Kjeringholmen peninsula.
Public transit: 1.0 km, ~13 min
route in Google Maps
Transit steps: Same as walking - too short and direct for transit advantage.
Tickets: Adult NOK 200 | Children/youth under 18 FREE | Family discount available. Considered "Norway's best museum" by Lonely Planet. World's largest drill bit, simulated rigs. Combine with the Geopark playground next door for a full half-day.

Gamle Stavanger (Old Stavanger)

Website: https://www.fjordnorway.com/en/see-and-do/gamle-stavanger
Google Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Gamle%20Stavanger%2C%20%C3%98vre%20Strandgate%2C%204005%20Stavanger
Open: Open 24/7. Free to wander the streets.
Walk: 0.6 km
route in Google Maps
Walk steps: Up Lokkeveien, right at Henrik Steffens gate, through Olav Kyrres Gate and the small Tidegeilen alley, into the white wooden-house district via Haugvaldstads gate to Ovre Strandgate.
Public transit: 0.6 km, ~9 min
route in Google Maps
Transit steps: Same as walking - direct walking distance.
Tickets: Free. 173 white wooden houses from late 1700s, all preserved. Cobblestone lanes, flower boxes, working artisan workshops. The IDDIS / Norsk Hermetikkmuseum (Canning Museum) is inside this district. Best at golden hour.

Walk: 0.5 km
route in Google Maps

Public transit: 0.5 km, ~7 min
route in Google Maps
Transit steps: Same as walking - direct walking distance.
Tickets: Adult NOK 75 | Children FREE. Norway's oldest cathedral (consecrated 1125), only Norwegian cathedral in continuous use since the 1300s. Recently restored. Quick 30-min visit.

Lysefjord Cruise (Rodne Fjord Cruise)

Website: https://rodne.no/en/fjord-experiences/stavanger/fjordcruise-lysefjord-preikestolen/
Google Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=R%C3%B8dne%20Fjord%20Cruise%2C%20Skagenkaien%2018%2C%204006%20Stavanger
Open: Daily departures in summer. Typical summer schedule: 10:00 and 12:00 daily, more on weekends. Verify on rodne.no. Cruise duration 3-3.5 hours.
Walk: 0.8 km
route in Google Maps
Walk steps: Down Lokkeveien, left on Arne Rettedals gt through Haakon VIIs gate, down Kongsgardbakken to Skagenkaien - terminal at no. 35-37, near Ovre Holmegate roundabout.
Public transit: 0.8 km, ~11 min
route in Google Maps
Transit steps: Same as walking.
Tickets: Adult ~NOK 690 | Child 4-15 ~NOK 350 | Under 4 free. PRE-BOOK ONLINE - peak August departures sell out. 3-3.5 hour electric catamaran with panoramic windows + onboard cafe. View Preikestolen (Pulpit Rock) FROM BELOW the rock face - see the entire fjord without the hike. Fantahala cave + waterfalls + Lysefjord bridge. Perfect for a 4yo (just sit and look) and 13yo (the views are spectacular).

Mosvatnet lake

Website: https://www.fjordnorway.com/en/see-and-do/mosvatnet
Google Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mosvatnet%2C%204011%20Stavanger
Open: Open 24/7. Free.
Walk: 1.6 km
route in Google Maps
Walk steps: Down Lokkeveien, around the roundabout onto Madlaveien, left on Torbjorn Hornkloves gate, follow Furras gate through residential streets, end on Folke Bernadottes Vei at the lake.
Public transit: 3.1 km, ~23 min
route in Google Maps
Transit steps: Walk 5 min to Vaktapoteket bus stop, take bus toward Forus via Stavanger (4 stops) to Valandsbakken, then 12 min walk to the lake. Walking is faster.

Bjergsted Park

Website: n/a
Google Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bjergstedparken%2C%204007%20Stavanger
Open: Open 24/7. Free.
Walk: 1.1 km
route in Google Maps
Walk steps: Up Lokkeveien (northwest), continue onto Tanke Svilands gate, around the roundabout, then onto Bjergsted Terrasse. Park near the Stavanger Concert Hall.
Public transit: 1.1 km, ~5 min
route in Google Maps
Transit steps: Walk 1 min to Ledaalsgata bus stop, take bus toward Randaberg (3 stops, 2 min), 2 min walk to the park.
Tickets: Free. Stavanger's largest leisure area near the city center, expanded toward the sea when the concert hall was built. Grassy lawns, walking paths, picnic spots. Adjacent to Stavanger Konserthus and the University of Stavanger Bjergsted campus.
