Attractions

Lighthouse (Latarnia Morska)

Website: n/a
Google Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Latarnia+Morska+Kolobrzeg
Open: Daily in season ~10:00–18:00 (longer in summer). 109 gentle steps up the 26 m tower for harbour views; inside there's a "Pirate Adventure" for kids and a minerals museum.
Walk: 2.0 km
route in Google Maps
Walk steps: A ~27-minute walk north to the port (or drive/park near the marina).
Tickets: ~10–15 PLN; the kids' Pirate Adventure may be a separate small fee.

Pier (Molo) & Central Beach

Website: n/a
Google Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Molo+Kolobrzeg
Open: Open access. One of Poland's longest concrete piers, with Baltic views; the wide central beach runs right alongside — sandcastles and paddling for the 4-year-old.
Walk: 1.8 km
route in Google Maps
Walk steps: A ~24-minute walk north to the seafront and pier.
Tickets: Free.

Pirate-Ship Harbour Cruise

Website: n/a
Google Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Rejsy+statkiem+pirackim+Kolobrzeg+port
Open: Seasonal (approx. May–Sep). ~40-minute themed cruises on a pirate (or torpedo) ship with crew animations — a guaranteed kids' highlight. Depart from the passenger port near the lighthouse.
Walk: ~1.8 km to the port quays (near the lighthouse)
route in Google Maps
Tickets: ~30–40 PLN adult, less for children; buy at the quay.

Museum of Polish Arms (Muzeum Oręża Polskiego)

Website: http://muzeum.kolobrzeg.pl/
Google Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Muzeum+Oreza+Polskiego+Kolobrzeg
Open: May–Jun & Sep daily 09:00–16:00; Jul–Aug daily 09:00–18:00. Weapons, armour, catapults and cannons across the ages — plus military vehicles. A big hit with the 13-year-old.
Walk: 0.2 km
route in Google Maps
Walk steps: A 3-minute walk from the town hall.
Tickets: Single museum 25 PLN / 20 reduced; two museums 50/40; free under 7.

Kołobrzeg Maritime Open-Air Museum (Skansen Morski)

Website: http://muzeum.kolobrzeg.pl/
Google Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kolobrzeski+Skansen+Morski
Open: Seasonal. Outdoor display of ships, boats and naval equipment at the port — great for clambering-eye kids; pairs naturally with the lighthouse and a cruise.
Walk: 1.7 km
route in Google Maps
Walk steps: A ~23-minute walk to the port on Bałtycka.
Tickets: Modest fee; included in the two-museum combined ticket.

Co-Cathedral (Bazylika Mariacka)

Website: n/a
Google Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bazylika+Mariacka+Kolobrzeg
Open: Open to visitors outside services. A vast Gothic brick basilica with famously "leaning" columns and historic art, in the heart of the rebuilt old town by the neo-Gothic town hall.
Walk: 0.3 km
route in Google Maps
Walk steps: A 3-minute walk from the town hall.
Tickets: Free (donation).

Seaside Spa Park (Nadmorski Park Zdrojowy)

Website: n/a
Google Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Nadmorski+Park+Zdrojowy+Kolobrzeg
Open: Open access. The green seaside spa park behind the beach — playgrounds, an amphitheatre and a rope/zipline park nearby (over the sea), with the brine-spa promenade.
Walk: 1.6 km
route in Google Maps
Walk steps: A ~23-minute walk to the seaside park by the pier.
Tickets: Park free; rope park paid per course.

Seaside Amusement Park (Lunapark)

Website: n/a
Google Maps: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Lunapark+Kolobrzeg
Open: Seasonal, daily in summer (afternoons/evenings). Carousels, inflatable playgrounds, small roller coasters and rides for all ages near the seafront.
Walk: 1.7 km
route in Google Maps
Walk steps: A ~23-minute walk toward the eastern seafront.
Tickets: Pay per ride/wristband.
